Throwable class is the "superclass" of all errors and exceptions in Java. They are thrown by the Java Virtual machine, or by the throw statement. Two subclasses are error and exception, which are used to indicate the exceptional situations have occured. A throwable contains a snapshot of the execution stack of its thread at the time is was created. One reason that a throwable may have a cause is that the class that throws it is built atop a lower layered abstraction; or the method to throws it must confor, tp a gerneral purpsose interface that does not permit the mehtod to throw the cause directly.
